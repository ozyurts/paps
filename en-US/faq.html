We answer your questions

Find answers to the most commonly asked questions about the Peer Support program below.

Peer Support is a program where trained employees provide confidential and safe support to their colleagues. It does not replace professional therapy, but serves as a valuable resource for initial support and referrals.

Yes, all conversations are conducted under a 100% confidentiality principle. Peers do not share conversation content with managers or third parties. Limited exceptions may apply only in cases of serious safety threats as required by law.

All Pegasus employees (cockpit, cabin, technical, office, etc.) can apply to the program. You can apply for yourself or make an anonymous referral for a colleague.

After your application is received, you will typically be matched with a suitable peer within 24-48 hours. The process can be expedited in urgent cases.

Peers are not professional psychologists or therapists, but they are specially trained and certified employees. When needed, they can refer you to professional support resources.

You can get support for work stress, burnout, personal difficulties, grief, work-life balance, team communication issues, and many other topics. Peers listen without judgment and work with you to find solutions.

Yes, the Peer Support program is completely free for all Pegasus employees.
